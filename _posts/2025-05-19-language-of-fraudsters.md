## Introduction
The modern-day fraudster has developed their own slang and terminology, and while this language helps threat actors communicate and conduct operations; It can be difficult and alien for an us “good guys” to understand.

Hopefully, this breakdown will help fill some of your knowledge gaps!
-----
## Terminology
Fullz: Full information, typically referring to a victim’s stolen bank card or identification.

Method: A way for a threat actor to conduct fraud, such as getting free designer clothing.

Proofs: Evidence of the legitimacy of a method, aka proving a method works.

Mainstream / Highstreet: Refers to Highstreet banks, such as Barclays and NatWest.

Logs: Text files containing compromised account details, such as a retail store victim’s login details.

Chop: Selling stolen bank accounts.

White cards: Cards loaded with a stolen bank account (The cards are commonly plain white).

F2F: Face to Face meeting, a fraudster meeting another person in real life.

Holder: An owner of an account, such as a bank account.

Loader: A person who puts money into an account, typically to conduct fraud.

Vishing: Conduct fraud through voice or calls.

SIM Swapping: A threat actor take control of a victim’s SIM card.

Addy: Address, this could be a real location or point of contact like an email address.

Mule: Someone to move dirty money through, allowing a threat actor to cash out more “safely”.

White names: English white names, commonly requested to conduct fraud attacks.

E-Whoring: Pretending to be a female online, tricking victims into sending money for adult content.

Pig Butchering: A scam designed to drain all the financial funds a victim holds. (Also known as “Sha Zhu Pan”).
